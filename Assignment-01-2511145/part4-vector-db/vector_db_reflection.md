## Vector DB Use Case

A traditional keyword-based database search would not suffice for a law firm needing to query 500-page contracts in plain English. Keyword searches rely on exact string matching or basic lexical variations (like stemming). If a lawyer searches for "What are the termination clauses?", a keyword system will explicitly look for words like "termination" or "clause." However, complex legal documents frequently use highly varied terminology. A contract might state, "This agreement shall be dissolved upon written notice," completely omitting the exact keywords. A traditional system would fail to retrieve this highly relevant section, leading to incomplete or inaccurate legal analysis.

This is precisely where a vector database becomes essential. Instead of storing just the raw text, a vector database stores mathematical representations of the text called embeddings. These embeddings capture the deep semantic meaning and context of the sentences, rather than just the raw characters. 

In this proposed system, the 500-page contracts would first be broken down into smaller chunks (like paragraphs) and converted into vector embeddings using a machine learning model. When a lawyer asks a plain English question, that query is also converted into a vector. The vector database then performs a mathematical similarity search (such as cosine similarity) to find the text chunks closest in meaning to the question. Consequently, the system will successfully recognize that "agreement shall be dissolved" is conceptually identical to a "termination clause," retrieving the exact information the lawyer needs regardless of the specific phrasing used.
